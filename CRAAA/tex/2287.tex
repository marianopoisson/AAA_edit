\abstract{
Understanding the fraction of ionizing photons that escape from star-forming galaxies and reach the intergalactic medium is crucial for explaining the re-ionization of intergalactic hydrogen, mostly finished by $z\sim6$. This is a parsec-scale process in the interstellar medium which has significant implications for the mega-parsec-scale intergalactic medium. However, no telescope can directly observe this radiation from objects at $z\geq6$ due to absorption by the neutral hydrogen during cosmic reionization. To investigate the physics behind the escape of ionizing photons produced by massive stars in young \hbox{H\,{\sc ii}} regions, we observed two compact \hbox{H\,{\sc ii}} regions in the Small Magellanic Cloud using the Integral Field Unit of GMOS-S. We analyze the optical emission line ratios, which are indicators of ionizing photon escape in high-redshift galaxies. At $\sim 61$ kpc, the proximity of these regions allows us to measure the gas geometry and kinematics with spatial resolution that is unattainable in reionization epoch galaxies. In this contribution, we present preliminary results for \hbox{H\,{\sc ii}} region SMC-N88A, including emission line ratio maps (\hbox{[O\,{\sc iii}]}/\hbox{[O\,{\sc ii}]}, \hbox{[O\,{\sc iii}]}/\hbox{H$\beta$}, \hbox{H$\beta$}/\hbox{H$\alpha$}, \hbox{[S\,{\sc iii}]}/\hbox{[S\,{\sc ii}]}) and integrated line fluxes.  We discuss the physical state of the \hbox{H\,{\sc ii}} region, the escape fraction of ionizing photons, and the applicability of these results to galaxies during the Epoch of Reionization.
}
