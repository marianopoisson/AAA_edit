\abstract{
The Small Magellanic Cloud (SMC) represents an ideal environment to study the interaction between molecular gas and dust under low-metallicity conditions ($\sim$20\% of the solar value). The low metallicity leads to a significant reduction in the abundance of molecular gas tracers such as carbon monoxide (CO), making its detection particularly challenging. Previous works have shown a good correlation between CO ($J=3\text{--}2$) emission, IR colors, and dust properties, indicating that CO emission in the SMC is a good tracer of star formation despite its weakness.

In this work, we present a comparative study between the molecular hydrogen (H$_2$) density and the physical and observational properties of interstellar dust obtained from public databases. We use observations of the CO ($J=2\text{--}1$) and ($J=3\text{--}2$) transitions observed with the Atacama Pathfinder Experiment (APEX) telescope to estimate H$_2$ column density maps in four regions within the SMC bar, at 40$^{\prime\prime}$ angular resolution ($\sim$12\,pc at the SMC’s distance), assuming local thermodynamic equilibrium (LTE) conditions. The mid-infrared (MIR) and far-infrared (FIR) dust emission were observed with the \textit{Spitzer} and \textit{Herschel} telescopes. Our comparison is complemented by archival dust parameter maps (density, visual extinction, and temperature). In the present study, we provide a calibration of the H$_2$ column density with both observational and physical dust properties. This calibration will help improve our understanding of the molecular gas and dust interaction, which is useful for theoretical and semi-analytic simulations.

}
